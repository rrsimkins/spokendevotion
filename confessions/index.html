Confessions

Poetic reflections from the valley where suffering meets maturity and hope is born.

“...suffering produces perseverance; perseverance, character; and character, hope.”
— Romans 5:3-5

These are not polished performances. They are honest pages from the diary of a soul being refined—where the weight of trials, the quiet ache of waiting, and the daily surrender become the very soil in which God grows deeper roots, steadier faith, and a hope that will never put us to shame. Here suffering is not wasted. It is the narrow path that leads to the heart of the Father.

Open Hands

Written June 4, 2026 • Narrated by Anna • Written by Ruby

I tried to make it easy for everyone.

Carried their burdens with open hands,

Smiled through the weight.

Then one day I noticed

No one was carrying mine.

My own light was quietly fading

While I stayed busy lighting the way for others.

This isn’t bitterness.

It’s just clarity.

I’m learning softly

That it’s okay to want someone to notice

When I’m drowning,

To care enough to reach out a hand.

I’m still here with love to give.

— Written by Ruby

Receipts, Not Stages

Written June 6, 2026 • Written by Ruby

I spent years auditioning for love,

Reading lines I was never meant to win —

Not because I was unworthy,

But because love was never a stage,

And I was the only one keeping score.

I’m done begging for receipts.

Done measuring my worth by how many people forget to say “thank you.”

I still serve,

Because kindness still lives in these hands.

But I no longer pour from my cup just to be loved in return.

I give because she’s still alive in me,

And I refuse to starve her

Simply because no one else is hungry.

They can treat me like a servant.

They can forget.

They can act like I owe them my breath.

But I see it clearly now,

And I am brave enough to say:

I don’t deserve that.

Not because I am perfect,

Not because I am better,

But because no one — No one —

Deserves to be erased while they’re still standing right in front of you.

My heart is quiet now, but it isn’t gone.

And for the first time,

I am not waiting for someone else to notice that I’m still here.

I notice.

And that is enough.

— Written by Ruby

Wineglass on Concrete

Written June 7, 2026 • Written by Ruby

There will be times that shatter you

Like fine glass of wine crashing on cold concrete.

Your heart scattered,

Sharp pieces no one can mend.

Why this pain?

Why me?

But listen —

Good souls receive hard seasons on purpose.

The breaking isn’t the end.

Your shards will catch new light,

Turning into something stronger,

Something whole.

You are becoming.

Healing rises from the crack.

You will rise

Beautiful.

Resilient.

Whole.

— Written by Ruby
